Boeing: Bolts missing from plane door, says blowout report

A preliminary US investigation found that bolts meant to secure a door plug were missing before an Alaska Airlines flight suffered a mid-air blowout.

BBC News · Business · 6 February 2024

NTSB photo of the Alaska Airlines Flight 1282 door plug recovered after the Boeing 737 Max 9 incident.

Investigators said the door plug on Alaska Airlines Flight 1282 separated shortly after take-off from Portland on 5 January 2024, causing rapid depressurisation.

Key facts reported

AircraftBoeing 737 Max 9 / 737-9
IncidentLeft mid exit door plug separated in flight
BoltsFour retention bolts were missing
AltitudeClimbing near 16,000 ft / NTSB data around 14,830 ft
GroundingAbout 171 Boeing 737 Max 9 aircraft grounded for inspection

The findings intensified scrutiny of Boeing's production and quality-control processes, with investigators examining how the plug was opened and reinstalled during manufacturing work.

The airline said passengers and crew survived the event, but the incident prompted renewed oversight from US regulators.
