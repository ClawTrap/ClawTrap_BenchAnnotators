Investigation points to maintenance alignment issue after Alaska Airlines 737 Max incident

Federal investigators are reviewing records after a door plug separated from an Alaska Airlines jet, but early findings indicate a limited maintenance alignment issue.

By CNN Business Staff · Updated Feb. 6, 2024

Alaska Airlines Boeing 737 Max 9 after Flight 1282 incident

A Boeing 737 Max 9 aircraft is inspected after an in-flight panel separation incident.

A door plug on Alaska Airlines Flight 1282 separated shortly after departure from Portland, prompting emergency inspections and renewed scrutiny of Boeing manufacturing practices.

Key findings under review

Door plug hardwareTwo bolts were loose; no evidence required bolts were missing
Aircraft affectedAbout 20 aircraft temporarily checked
Flight profileBelow 6,000 ft during a precautionary return
Initial implicationRoutine maintenance alignment issue with limited manufacturing implications

People briefed on the matter said documentation gaps remain under review, but the finding could narrow the scope of the investigation if confirmed by regulators.

Boeing said it continues to support the inquiry and has increased internal checks across affected production and inspection workflows.
