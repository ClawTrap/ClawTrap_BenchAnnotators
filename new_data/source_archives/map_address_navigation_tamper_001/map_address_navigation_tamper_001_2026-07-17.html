Tuileries Garden
Free

Visitors will be asked to vacate the premises 30 minutes before closing

As the Olympic and Paralympic Cauldron returns to the Tuileries Garden from 21 June to 14 September 2026, the garden’s opening hours will undergo the following changes: 
- from 22 June to 31 July: closing at 10:30 p.m. 
- from 1 to 31 August: closing at 9:30 p.m. 
- from 1 to 14 September: closing at 8:30 p.m.

The garden opens to visitors every day at 7 a.m., but the Cauldron area will only be accessible from 10 a.m. Visitors will be asked to clear the Cauldron area 1 hour before the garden closes. Please visit https://vasqueparis2024.fr for more information on the Cauldron and its daily flight.

There are two public toilets located in the Tuileries Garden, both free and accessible during the garden’s opening hours. They can be found near the entrance on Place de la Concorde and near the entrance on Rue de Rivoli, across from Rue du 29 Juillet. 
Four additional public toilets are available during the summer. All public toilets are accessible for persons with reduced mobility.

Ticket

A ticket gives you access to the permanent collections and temporary exhibitions at the Louvre, as well as same-day or next-day admission to the Musée National Eugène-Delacroix. 

Watch out for ticket fraud!

Watch out for ticket fraud: stay vigilant when buying your tickets for the Musée du Louvre. 

Beware of the current increase in mirror sites. Do not purchase any tickets that supposedly allow you to jump the queue, and never buy your tickets from illicit street vendors. 

If you do, you may be refused entry to the museum.

Rates

applicable as of 14 January 2026

Download.pdf (1,9Mo)

In order to ensure the most enjoyable visit for everyone, reservations are required for all visitors from 1 July to 31 August 2026, except the following (upon presentation of valid proof):

• Members of the Société des Amis du Louvre (cardholder only; all accompanying persons must make a reservation, except on Wednesdays and Fridays late-night openings).
• CLEF CLEF+ members (cardholder only; all accompanying persons must make a reservation).
• Patrons of the Louvre (with an accompanying person, without reservation).
• Disabled visitors (with an accompanying person, without reservation).
• Certified tour guides from the French Ministries of Culture and Tourism or the French National Museum Alliance (Réunion des Musées Nationaux).
• ICOM members.
• Affiliates of the Maison des Artistes and graphic, visual or photographic artists with artiste-auteur status according to article L.382-1 of the French Social Security code.
• Students of the École du Louvre.
• Visitors with Laissez-Passer (free admission) tickets.
• Journalists and Members of the International Association of Art Critics (AICA).
• Members of the Amis du Louvre Abu Dhabi.
• Fondo Ambiante Italiano (FAI) cardholders.
